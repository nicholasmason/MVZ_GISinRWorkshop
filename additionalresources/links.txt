### GIS resources at Berkeley ###
Geospatial Innovation Facility (GIF), http://gif.berkeley.edu
	Offers regular workshops, office hours advising (need to sign up), GIS workstations.  Can subscribe to "gif-community@lists.berkeley.edu” to keep advised of GIS related events.

ESPM 137 – Landscape Ecology (Fall semesters, taught by Ian Wang)
	Goes through a variety of GIS techniques and using R for GIS analyses.  I highly recommend the class!

The Berkeley Ecoinformatics Engine  https://ecoengine.berkeley.edu/
Aims to provide access to biodiversity information from the university’s rich and diverse repositories as well as tools to promote visualization and in depth analysis. 
Has an R package that lets you easily plot species occurrence data from museum databases.  https://ropensci.org/blog/2014/01/30/ecoengine/

MVZ maproom
	Has workstations with GIS software.

QGIS: A Free and Open Source Geographic Information System 
	https://www.qgis.org/en/site/
